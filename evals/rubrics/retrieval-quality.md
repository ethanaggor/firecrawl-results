# Retrieval quality rubric

Use this rubric after completing a trajectory. Score each dimension from 1 to 5.

## Relevance

5: Returned evidence directly answers the task.
4: Returned evidence answers most of the task.
3: Returned evidence is partially relevant but incomplete.
2: Returned evidence is loosely related.
1: Returned evidence is irrelevant.

## Source quality

5: Primary/official sources dominate the evidence.
4: Mostly authoritative sources with minor secondary material.
3: Mixed source quality.
2: Mostly secondary or low-authority sources.
1: Source quality is poor or unclear.

## Coverage

5: Evidence covers all requested facts and edge cases.
4: Evidence covers most requested facts.
3: Evidence covers the main point but misses important detail.
2: Evidence is thin.
1: Evidence is missing.

## Freshness

5: Evidence is current when the task requires current information.
4: Evidence is probably current but not explicitly dated.
3: Freshness is unclear.
2: Some evidence appears stale.
1: Evidence is stale for the task.

## Grounding

5: Final answer can be traced directly to visible evidence.
4: Most claims are grounded.
3: Important claims need inference or missing support.
2: Evidence weakly supports the answer.
1: Answer is not supported by visible evidence.

## Overall score

Average the dimensions, then add a short rationale that references exact trace steps and URLs.
